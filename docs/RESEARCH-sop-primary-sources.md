## Executive summary

The canonical SOP backbone for Indian small/mid contracting is the **CPWD works-accounting chain: Measurement Book (MB) → Running Account (RA) bill → retention/security-deposit recovery → final bill**, governed by the CPWD Works Manual and the CPWA Code's statutory forms. The MB is the single legal basis for every payment (Form 23); RA bills follow a fixed **Memorandum-of-Payments** reconciliation (Form 26) with security deposit deducted at 2.5% per bill on top of a 5% performance guarantee; estimation builds up from the CPWD Analysis of Rates (material + labour + sundries → +1% water → +15% CPOH → per-unit). **For works ≥ ₹15 lakh, contractor-prepared Computerised Measurement Books are officially sanctioned — MB-format software output is an accepted deliverable, directly validating Contractor-OS's core proposition.** Internationally, the Last Planner System supplies the production-planning SOP (four-level planning, weekly commitments, PPC metric) and FIDIC's 2021 Green Book the small-works contract form.

## Verified findings (all high-confidence)

### A. Measurement & billing spine (the core)
1. **The MB is the single legal basis for all quantity accounting and every payment** — RA/final bills may only be paid against MB-recorded measurements; the MB is court-admissible (CPWD Works Manual Para 7.2; CPWA Form 23). 3-0 across four primary sources.
2. **MB record-integrity SOP** — each set opens with header fields (work name, location, contractor, agreement no./date, dates of commencement/completion/measurement, ref to previous measurements); entries in ink, never erased (corrections crossed out, initialed, dated); machine-numbered pages, no blanks/torn; contractor signature per set; dated "Measured by me" certificate. Tiered check: 100% by AE, 10% by Divisional Officer (Para 7.5; CPWA Form 23, cols: Particulars, No., Length, Breadth, Depth, Contents/Area).
3. **Works ≥ ₹15 lakh: contractor-prepared Computerised MBs (CMBs)** on A-4 machine-numbered pages, same MB format, measurements carried forward, hard copy to department (Works Manual Para 7.12). *Officially sanctions MB-format software output.*
4. **Two payment formats** — First & Final Bill (Form 24, single completion payment) and Running Account Bill (Form 26, staged). Every RA bill carries: serial no., previous-bill reference, agreement no., dates of written order to commence + actual completion, and a certificate citing the exact **MB number and page** of the billed quantities.
5. **RA-bill Memorandum of Payments arithmetic** — measured work value (A) + advances − withheld = up-to-date payment (K); deduct previous bill's K; settle net by **recoveries [(i) Taxes/TDS (ii) Security Deposit (iii) Other]** + balance by cheque. Retention is a recovery line *within* each bill.
6. **Retention/security regime** — 5% performance guarantee of tendered value before commencement, **plus** 2.5% security deposit deducted from gross of every running + final bill; releasable against bank guarantee once ≥ ₹5 lakh (Paras 21.1, 21.2). *State PWDs vary — e.g. Arunachal 10%/bill.*
7. **Payment timelines** — running bills ~6 working days (Works Manual target); final bill submitted within 1 month of completion certificate; final bills payable in 2/3/6 months by value band, 7.5% p.a. interest on delay. *Caveat: GCC 2019 Clause 8 supersedes with 10th-working-day payment + 5% p.a. on delayed intermediate bills — cite the governing contract, not just the Manual.*
8. **RA-bill quality gates** — proforma of mandatory tests attached to each bill; all test registers + Material-at-Site (MAS) register must accompany **every alternate RA bill and the final bill**, else payment withheld; theoretical material-consumption statement accompanies every bill (SOP to Works Manual 2019). *Links QC testing to billing readiness.*

### B. Labour (informal reality)
9. **Muster Roll (Form 21) is two-part** — Part I nominal roll (name + father's name, daily attendance, rate, amount, payee signature/thumb impression **at payment**); Part II measurement of work done by that labour vs MB, excess/saving explained. Unpaid wages struck off → **Register of Unpaid Wages (Form 21A)**. *Canonical muster/payout format for informal labour.*

### C. Estimation & BOQ
10. **CPWD DAR is the canonical rate basis** — scientifically-assessed material/labour/machinery inputs, maintained since 1950. Rates anchored to dated inputs (Delhi materials Mar-Apr 2014; labour = Delhi statutory minimum wages 01.04.2014). *Ties labour cost to minimum wages, not market. Time-sensitive — must be updatable to current DSR/state SoR.*
11. **Rate-analysis skeleton** — material + trade-wise labour (Mason/Coolie/Bhisti person-days) + lump-sum scaffolding/sundries → **+1% water charges → +15% CPOH (7.5% profit + 7.5% overhead)** → ÷ measurement unit. *Water/scaffolding apply to wet/access items only — per-item toggles, not hard-coded.*
12. **Sub-head taxonomy** — numbered chapters 00 Basic Rates, 02 Earth Work, 03 Mortars, 04 Concrete, 05 RCC, 06 Brick Work … 13 Finishing, 16 Road Work, 17 Sanitary, 18 Water Supply, 19 Drainage, 20 Pile Work, 22 Water Proofing, 26 Structural Glazing, with hierarchical item codes (13.1.1 refs mortar 3.4). *De facto BOQ chapter/numbering convention; state SoRs vary.*

### D. International best practice
13. **Last Planner System** — four-level planning (master/CPM → collaborative phase → lookahead + constraints → weekly work plan), five conversations (should/can/will/did/learn); weekly plan = explicit commitments from the specific people doing the work.
14. **PPC metric** — Percent Plan Complete = actual completions ÷ weekly assignments, with Five-Whys root-cause on every miss. Traditional planning ~54% baseline; LPS targets 80%+.
15. **FIDIC Short Form of Contract 2nd ed. (2021 Green Book)** — updated small-works contract (lump-sum / re-measurement / cost-reimbursable). *CPWD GCC still dominates Indian public works.*

## Caveats
- **Time-sensitivity:** DAR rates are 2014-dated; a live estimation module must let contractors update to current DSR/state SoR, not ship 2014 rates as authoritative.
- **GCC vs Manual:** GCC 2019 supersedes some Works-Manual terms (notably payment timelines). Where the app prints/enforces timelines, cite the governing contract.
- **State-PWD variance:** security-deposit %, sub-head lists, and bill formats differ by state. Encode CPWD as defaults with configurable overrides.
- **Coverage gaps:** thin on safety/BOCW specifics, procurement detail beyond MAS, and GST/TDS mechanics beyond the RA-bill "Taxes" line (see open questions).
